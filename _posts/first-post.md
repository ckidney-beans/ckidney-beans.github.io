#Return to an old interest with a renewed passion.

The other weekend, I was at my parents house for a family gathering to celebrate one of my brother's birthdays.  During conversation with my 18 niece, I learned that teens these days have gone back to carrying digital cameras with them.  A trend that I gave up with around the timeline of the release and purchase of my first iPhone.  Outside of a period of a few years where I was invested in the hobby of photography, with an focus on widelife photography, I truly never felt the need to carry a extra device for just day to day photos.  Butt, as I have also learned from my niece that low raise jeans are back (something my wife is not fond of), it seems a number of trends from the early 2000's are returning.  So, I thought, why not blogging!

I am probably aging myself right off the bat on this blog, with the pure use of the word blog.  But that's ok, cause one of the things I am learning about aging is that you need to accept that you are aging.

If you haven't already figured it out, I am not sure what the total content of this site is going to be.  I am a day to day professional in the insurance industry with a life long love for electrics, computers, engineering, and science.  I have recently discovered the world of Homelabs and I have taken on the tasks of learning, installing, maintaining, and running my own homelab environment.  There is no end goal here, no finish line to cross or acheivements to meet.  I am looking at this as a source of accomplishment, but don't want to put the pressure of goals or timelines on myself.  I am, after all, in no ways a professional or expert in the topics that I will address on this site.

That needs to be clear, I am no professional.  I am going to misunderstand things, I am going to make errors, I am going to need to go back to recreate or edit things that I have already done, I am going to need to delete things and start over, I am going to stumble.  But as a sign that is posted on the wall of my mainfloor bathroom states, "when you stumble, make it part of the dance" (my wife is a dancer), these misteps are just going to show my future self just how much I have learned.

And I guess that is the goal, if any.  To learn.  It's kinda been the goal of my life.  I have always had that curiosty, that engineers mind, the need to know how everything works.  From a computer or network system, to love of theoertical physics (where I understand nothing but love the mind exercise).  The other thing that is going to be a feature of this site are comments, thoughts, and updates on my other interests, which currently include 3D printing, ethical hacking, and motor sports / sim racing, especially F1.  But there will be more on those topics in the future.

One of the best ways to learn something is to roll up your sleeves and get your hands dirty.  This is the point of this site, to document the messy moments of someone who has only an amateur hobbiest's level of experience in a topic learn his way through and build something that he can use.

I guess I might as well give some details of where we are at the start of this matter, cause sadly this is not the bottom floor enterance.  I have been working on my current homelab for a few weeks now, with most of that time just learning.  Currently I am running a HP Slim Desktop operating a Proxmox services that is currently hosting my own RSS cleint, using FreshRSS.  I currently have a two subnet network in my home, one hosting my Roger's TV service and the other hosting my main home network.  There will be more detail on these items in the future, as I plan on making additional changes to the network to segment it for oranization, as well as for additional security.

Next plans for my network is going to looking into a homelab monitoring or dashboard application that I can locally host and keep an eye on the status of my services, as I start to expand my homelab.  Early thoughts into this topic are leaning towards using Home Assistant for this function, as I do already plan on having it as a focus of my home network.  Issue there is that the final plans are for my ioT network to be hosted on its own VLan from my main services network.
